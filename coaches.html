Coaches

BENGI

Bengi, a legendary figure in T1's history, returned as head coach in 2022 and played a pivotal role in revitalizing the team's performance. Under his guidance, T1 showcased consistency throughout the year, culminating in an LCK title and a strong showing at Worlds. His steady leadership helped lay the groundwork for T1’s ongoing success before his resignation after the 2022 season.

KKOMA

Kkoma returned to T1 in 2024 after a seven-year absence, having left the organization in 2019 to pursue opportunities with other teams and international ventures. As T1's head coach during their golden era (2012–2019), Kkoma led the team to three Worlds titles and multiple domestic championships. His comeback in 2024 was hailed as a significant reunion, aiming to rebuild T1’s dominance and guide the team with his unparalleled strategic expertise and experience.

ROACH

As an assistant coach, Roach provided valuable insights and support to T1's roster, contributing significantly to their preparations during critical matches. His analytical approach and dedication to the team's wellbeing were instrumental in the team’s strategies. Roach departed after Worlds 2024 to fulfill his civil duty services, leaving behind a legacy of professionalism and expertise.

SKY

Sky joined T1’s coaching staff with a strong understanding of mid-lane dynamics and contributed significantly to the team’s growth throughout his tenure. He played an essential role in T1's strategic successes leading up to Worlds 2023. After the tournament, Sky had to leave the organization to fulfill his military service obligations.

TOM

Following Bengi’s departure, Tom stepped up as T1’s head coach, ensuring continuity and stability during a critical period for the team. Known for his analytical mindset and calm approach, Tom focused on refining T1's coordination and individual strengths. Under his leadership in 2023, T1 achieved key victories and maintained their reputation as a world-class team. His ability to adapt to high-pressure situations cemented his role as a vital figure in T1's coaching staff during a transitional phase.
